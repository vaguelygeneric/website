Welcome to The Generic. We're glad you found us, mostly because we're not sure how you would.

In this first episode we spend a surprising amount of time on introductions — who we are, why we're doing this, and what, if anything, this show is supposed to be about. The answer to that last question remains somewhat unclear by the end of the episode, which felt right.

We also somehow get into a twenty-minute discussion about whether ambient noise in coffee shops is annoying or calming, and what that preference reveals about a person. Spoiler: neither of us agrees on this, which is probably a good sign for the show's future.

**Topics covered (loosely):**

- Who we are and why we're doing this
- The difficulty of describing a podcast that doesn't have a topic
- Coffee shop noise: for or against?
- What makes a good conversation worth recording
- Whether "nothing specific" is actually a premise

Thanks for listening to episode one. We promise (loosely) that future episodes will be more focused. Or at least equally unfocused in a more intentional way.
