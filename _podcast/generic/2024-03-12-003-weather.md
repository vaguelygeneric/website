Everyone talks about the weather. We decided to actually talk about it.

What started as a bit — "let's do the most generic possible episode" — turned into a surprisingly real conversation about why small talk exists, why it's underrated, what it does socially, and why weather specifically has been the universal icebreaker across basically every culture.

Also: the ethics of complaining. Is constant weather-complaining a net negative on group mood, or is it a low-stakes bonding ritual? We have opinions that conflict.

**What we cover:**

- Why weather is the universal small-talk topic and what that says about us
- The social function of complaining (genuinely interesting, we promise)
- Whether people who "don't do small talk" are onto something or are just rude
- Seasonal affective disorder and how much the weather actually controls your mood
- The most dramatic weather either of us has ever personally experienced (one of these stories is better than the other)
