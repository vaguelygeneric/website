We started this episode with a plan to talk about productivity. We ended it somewhere in the philosophy of attention and a digression about aquariums. Classic.

The original question was simple: does having a hundred browser tabs open actually hurt your ability to think, or is it just a different organizational style that gets unfairly judged? We answer this in about four different directions.

Along the way we get into whether the printing press caused the same anxieties about information overload that smartphones do now (it did), whether "focus" is a skill you can train or a personality trait (unclear), and what it says about us that we find a fish tank calming.

**Things discussed:**

- The psychology of tab hoarding
- Historical panics about too much information (the printing press, the telegraph, TV...)
- Attention as a resource vs. attention as a practice
- Why aquariums are peaceful and what that means
- Apps that are supposed to help you focus and whether any of them actually work

No conclusions were harmed in the making of this episode.
